# RALF Storytelling Plan

*Research compiled February 2026 via multi-agent collaboration (MediaPipe expert, Gesture Studio architect, art/tech landscape researcher, storytelling strategist).*

---

## The Story

RALF's story isn't "I built cool gesture recognition software." The story is:

> A house music DJ and capoeirista built a relational instrument that makes 70,000 years of embodied intelligence audible — using nothing but a webcam.

This positions RALF uniquely in a landscape where McGregor uses AI to *generate* choreography, Mi.Mu sells $2,500 gloves, Wekinator is a general-purpose toolkit, and dance digitization projects treat movement as *data to archive*. RALF does none of these. It **listens to movement and responds musically, in real time, grounded in specific lineage knowledge.**

### The Inversion That Matters

Most dance+tech projects frame technology as the innovator and dance as material. RALF inverts this — dance is the 70,000-year-old intelligent system, technology is the listener. Every piece of content should embody this: show the body first, then reveal the technology serving it.

## The Vehicle: Video + Website (Modular)

The **video comes first** — it serves 90% of audiences with minor re-cuts. The website is a frame for the video plus modular depth for different visitors.

### Phase 1: Demo Video (3 minutes) — Highest Priority

1. **0:00–0:30 — The Hook** (emotion first)
   - You moving. Music responding. No explanation. Just the experience.
   - Skeleton overlay appears subtly — "this is how the machine sees"

2. **0:30–1:30 — The Training Loop** (human-AI collaboration)
   - Teaching a gesture: countdown → CAPTURING → rest → repeat
   - "I'm teaching the machine what a 'jack' looks like"
   - Show 5 examples being recorded — the structured flow-state workflow

3. **1:30–2:15 — The Recognition Moment** (the payoff)
   - Perform the gesture freely → DTW distance drops → HIT fires → music responds
   - Split screen: body + Gesture Studio monitor + Ableton reacting
   - The "holy shit" moment: the system *gets it*

4. **2:15–3:00 — The Why** (the dharma, briefly)
   - Voice-over: "These movement vocabularies aren't entertainment. They're design grammars — 70,000 years of embodied intelligence for coordinating humans under complexity. This technology makes that intelligence audible."
   - End on: someone who's never danced before, moving, making music, smiling.

**Re-cuts for different audiences:**
- Google/technical: Add architecture diagram overlay, mention MediaPipe + DTW
- Knight/NEA: Add community footage, cultural preservation angle
- Creative Mornings: Keep the 3-min version as-is, it's the talk opener

## Landscape Positioning

### How RALF Differs From Adjacent Projects

| Project | What They Do | How RALF Differs |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Wayne McGregor + Google** | AI generates new choreography from 25-year archive | RALF treats dance as the intelligent system, technology as listener |
| **Mi.Mu Gloves** | $2,500 wearable MIDI controllers | RALF uses webcam (zero hardware cost), culturally specific vocabularies |
| **Wekinator** | General-purpose ML toolkit for gesture | RALF is a performance instrument with flow-state UX and cultural grounding |
| **Daito Manabe / Klaus Obermaier** | Technology as "third performer" on stage | RALF positions technology as accompanist, not peer |
| **Motion Bank / OpenEndedGroup** | Digitizing dance for archival/study | RALF activates lineage knowledge in real-time performance, not preservation |
| **FreeMoCap** | Open-source mocap infrastructure | RALF is what you perform with after capturing — potential integration |
| **IRCAM Gesture Follower** | Temporal pattern matching for music interaction | RALF adds cultural specificity and the four-system mapping model |
| **Genki Wave / Mictic** | Wearable motion → MIDI controllers | RALF is software-only, culturally grounded, webcam-based |

### RALF's Unique Position

1. **Embodied-first, not technology-first** — dance is the intelligence, technology listens
2. **Performance instrument, not research prototype** — complete system for live shows
3. **Culturally specific, not general-purpose** — Chicago/Detroit house + capoeira vocabularies
4. **Accessible hardware, sophisticated software** — webcam-based, sophistication in recognition
5. **Lineage transmission, not archival preservation** — activates embodied knowledge in present tense
6. **70,000-year framing** — reframes who the experts are and what's at stake

## Technical Talking Points (By Audience)

### For Engineers (Google, MIT)
- VAD-style state machine borrowed from speech recognition → 0% echo rate
- Three-layer DTW optimization: LB_Keogh + Sakoe-Chiba band + early abandoning
- Statistical threshold calibration (μ+σ) adapts to gesture variability
- FAIR-compliant .ralf format with UUID, tracking system metadata, license fields
- Preprocessing pipeline: hip centering, scale normalization, velocity features

### For Artists (Dance, Performance, Creative Mornings)
- Flow-state friendly training: audio feedback, no text during capture, structured sessions
- Four-system model (streams/stacks/intents/signals) as compositional vocabulary
- Portable gesture vocabularies: share .ralf files like MIDI files
- Replaces Wekinator with tool designed for dancers, not programmers

### For Funders (Knight, NEA, Mozilla)
- Open, interoperable architecture (OSC, JSON, FAIR principles)
- Zero hardware cost (webcam-based via MediaPipe)
- Bridges art and technology with cultural preservation
- Research-ready data format enables computational musicology

## What NOT to Do

- Don't build a complex website before the video exists. The video *is* the website.
- Don't lead with technical architecture for non-technical audiences. Lead with the experience.
- Don't use "dismember/re-member" language without testing it. Powerful but risks sounding academic. Let the video make the concept visceral *before* the words appear.
- Don't position as competing with Wekinator or Mi.Mu. Different category: "relational instrument grounded in lineage knowledge."
- Don't wait for the software to be "finished." v0.7.1 with 151 passing tests is more than enough to demo.
