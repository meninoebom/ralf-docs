
## On What RALF Does
- "You are live remixing a piece of music with your own movement. It is mutually transformative dialogue."
- "The dancer says 'Hey RALF, here is what I am feeling in response to the music in this moment, as interpreted by my body's motion.' And RALF says 'Well that aligns with this sound, my interpretation of that movement makes me want to generate this sound.'"
- "What you get is an emergent universe that the dancer gets to inhabit and play in."

## On African Diasporic Roots
- "In the African diaspora, dance and music often are part of a dialogical relationship — they exist in relationship to each other, they are flexible, adaptive, responsive, in conversation."
- "Bringing that West African drum circle spontaneity into the fine arts."
- "The flash of the spirit has to happen in real time — and that is where I find myself working with technologies that support real-time conversational exchanges between moving bodies and music."

## On Technologies of Remembering
- "Dancing is a technology for remembering yourself, for repair, for coming back to your body after a week of being in your head."
- "We use art, religion, music and dance to re-member, reconnect ourselves."
- "These are techniques that came from seeing what worked, trial and error, being passed down and adapted through time."

## On Technology
- "I am using the master's tools to tear down the master's house."
- "Gay, Black and brown men in Chicago in the 1980s took the tools of the new drum machines and turntables and reconfigured how they were used to create a new kind of ritual and ceremony of remembering."
- "Enslaved Africans took the barrels of rum, which they filled with their work and blood, and turned them over and used them for steel drums — transformed them into technologies of remembering."
- "Instrument makers are craftspeople, but they are also technicians — pianos and guitars are highly precise — but the spirit that emerges, that they can carry, is so deep, profound, inspirational, timeless. It's possible to take anything and make it into the tool of spirit."
- "I don't 100% believe that it's a good thing to incorporate technology and AI into dance — but it feels to me that it's an urgent thing to explore because of the salience and power of this moment in history."

## On What Endures
- "The technology is shifting under my feet, but the thing I care about does not shift. The compelling part — that inter-relatedness of dancer and music, movement-led composing, the relationality of sound and motion — does not change."

## On Participation vs. Observation
- "You have to participate in reality to comprehend it — and be transformed by it."
- "Flamenco, Bomba, Vodun, Samba, Capoeira — all of those dance forms require you to participate in it and be transformed by it, and in that moment you will generate something."

## On the Cartesian Counter-Narrative
- "There is a conventional narrative that technology is driven by a western approach to science and technology — it is Cartesian, in the sense that reality can be understood with the right mathematical or scientific model. What I am interested in, which predates that way of thinking, is that you have to participate in reality to comprehend it."
