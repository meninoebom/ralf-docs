
## 1. Professional Dance Performance
Choreographer + composer + dancers collaborate to create a piece with narrative arc (e.g., grief). Composer builds reconfigurable sound library; movement is wired to musical responses through the scene editor. Every performance is unique — the dancers generate the score in real time while inhabiting the composer's musical world.

**Example partner:** Alonzo King LINES Ballet (mentioned by Brandon as illustrative)

## 2. Open Play / Public Installation
No training needed. A piece of music is computationally decomposed. Anyone walks up to a camera and explores the music through movement — "as if it was a kind of space." Music starts when you step in. Low barrier, high engagement. Could work as gallery installation, museum interactive, festival activation.

## 3. Physical Rehabilitation
Rhythm-based feedback for neurodegenerative conditions (gait training), arm mobility recovery, or general movement therapy. Scientific literature supports rhythmic feedback for walking. RALF makes rehab playful — patients choose music that motivates them, and their target movements (walking, arm raises) produce musical responses. Each patient gets a personalized scene.

## 4. Crowd / Participatory Performance
Multiple dancers with phones (IMU sensors) or cameras. RALF measures relational qualities between dancers — synchrony, contrast, aggregate energy. A virtual "crowd" entity emerges. Brings the participatory spatial architecture of African diasporic traditions into technologically-mediated performance.
